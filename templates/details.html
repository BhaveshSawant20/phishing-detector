🛡️ Phishing Website Details

Learn how phishing works and how to protect yourself

Phishing websites are malicious platforms designed to steal sensitive information such as login credentials, banking details, and personal data by pretending to be legitimate websites.

⚠️ Common Signs of Phishing

  • Suspicious domain names (e.g., paypaI.com instead of paypal.com)
  • Use of HTTP instead of HTTPS
  • Too many special characters or random strings in the URL
  • Fake login pages asking for urgent action
  • Spelling mistakes and poor page design
  • Unusual pop-ups or unexpected download prompts

🎣 Types of Phishing

  • Email Phishing: Fake emails pretending to be from trusted organizations.
  • Website Phishing: Cloned websites mimicking real sites to steal credentials.
  • Smishing: Phishing via SMS or text messages.
  • Vishing: Voice phishing using phone calls.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing towards specific individuals or companies.

💥 Consequences of Phishing

  • Unauthorized access to bank accounts or financial loss.
  • Identity theft and misuse of personal data.
  • Compromise of business or corporate accounts.
  • Installation of malware or ransomware on devices.

🔎 How to Verify a Website

  • Check the URL carefully for spelling errors or extra characters.
  • Look for HTTPS and a valid security certificate.
  • Search the domain on Google or check reputation services like VirusTotal.
  • Avoid clicking suspicious email links or pop-ups.
  • Use password managers — they automatically detect phishing domains.

🛡️ Prevention Tips

  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
  • Keep software, browsers, and OS updated regularly.
  • Educate yourself and team members about phishing tactics.
  • Use anti-phishing browser extensions and security software.
  • Never reuse passwords across multiple websites.

📰 Real-World Examples

  • PayPal phishing email asking to verify account with a fake link.
  • Netflix phishing site requesting login to continue subscription.
  • Banking phishing SMS pretending to be from official bank numbers.
